Anchoring

  1. Catting
    1. Prepare
      1. Get cat hook from fo'c's'le, stored beside/beneath the ladder
      2. Free spoon and fish tackle from railing
      3. Hook cat hook to fluke ring
      4. Attach cat hook to fish tackle
      5. ?Ensure wildcat is IN gear
      6. ?Ensure brake is ON
      7. Top-down lead fish tackle lead on gypsy head
        This is like top down on capstan, leave out enough slack for 3 wraps then while holding the tail wrap from the tail towards the load then palm the wraps to remove slack.
        The load will be aft the windlass, same as the tail.
    2. all ready?
      1. check the cathead retaining pin
      2. release chain binder
      3. breast the anchor fluke off the deck (use the spoon!)
    3. ease away the fish tackle, you are done when anchor is hanging down from the cathead.
    4. disconnect ?and stow the fish tackle and spoon
  2. Letting Fall
    1. prepare
      1. clear the chain locker (of other people and anything that isn't the chain)
      2. clear the buckler plate (?tie it to something?)
      3. check the brake
      4. take wildcat OUT of gear
      5. ease the brake until just loose
      6. trigger person clips in with hammer secured to their wrist
      7. stand by bell to ring the shots
    2. "Let Fall"
      1. the hammer is used to free the pin on the cat head
      2. call out as each shot marker crosses the wildcat, bell gets rung with each.
  3. "Clean up the deck"
    1. stow fish tackle and spoon
    2. stow cat hook
    3. restore buckler plate

Weighing

  1. Haul short
    1. crew to chain locker, readies tools for flaking the chain
    2. install windlass bars
    3. heave until the pawls line up
    4. put wildcat into gear
  2. Raising
    1. release brake
    2. heave around windlass until chain is taught, expect some sail handling action at this time,
      ring the shots as they pass over the wildcat
    3. heave around until anchor is at hawse pipe
    4. remove windlass bars
    5. ready the capstan (the fore tack has been removed and set aside well in the past)
  3. Fishing
    1. route fish hook line to sheave on cathead, under the staple, and then to capstan
    2. load fish hook line on capstan and take out slack, ?top down
    3. hook the crown shackle
    4. heave capstan until line is strained
    5. take wildcat out of gear
    6. release the brake, expect 6 feet of chain to run out
    7. heave around capstan until eye of fish hook is at cathead
    8. keeper chain is run through the lifting ring
    9. insert trigger through the staple
    10. ease capstan until keeper chain has the weight
    11. remove fish hook from crown shackle and lead aft of cathead.
    12. stock should be alongside the hull, use a rope around the fluke to do any managing of this
  4. Stowing
    1. cat hook the fluke ring
    2. fish tackle to the windlass (gypsy head), top down lead.
    3. hook eye of cat hook to hook of fish tackle
    4. hold tension on the cat hook while taking the slack out of the fish tackle
    5. pump windlass, using spoon to keep anchor from catching on rail.
    6. when fluke is above rail ease the spoon and the fluke should settle down on its usual spot
    7. unship windlass bars
    8. secure chain binder around fluke
    9. remove fish tackle et alia and stow them
    10. check binder plate is in place
