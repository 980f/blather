knots

Reef / Square
gaskets on furled headsail
Blood
keep line from pulling out of block or fairlead
Figure 8
ditto Blood but for gantline where it needs to be easier to untie
Sheet Bend exit the loop over the bitter end of the bight temporary tie smaller line to heavier one to haul the heavier such as up into rigging
Double Sheet Bend
ditto Sheet Bend but when heavier line is slick or thick
Bowline bight alternates over and under mooring line, fix a line to a stanchion without it locking on to stanchion, tool lanyards
Cow Hitch
becket to jackstay, button for loose lines to shrouds, bitter end of shroud lanyards
Clove Hitch round turn and a half hitch ratlines to shrouds
Constrictor over the standing line before finishing the clove temporary whipping, slippery heaving line
Buntline clove on standing, round the outside first lines to edges of sail
Round Turn and 2 Half Hitches round and loop, clove on standing round the inside first temporary hitch, such as small craft to a ring.
Rolling Hitch
stopper such as on a halyard, as tent rope for awnings
Klemheist Hitch wrap from center to each end, load tucks into the other attach jigger tackle to a line
Gasket Coil end move is a cow hitch keep an otherwise loose line neat, yardarm gasket

Mouse a shackle
keep the pin from coming out
Mouse a cargo hook
keep the load from slipping off of the hook
Marline Hitch constrictor, overhands around thing, constrictor pull tighter on the bitter end of a line being tied
Marline ... loop forward, pick up standing line with spike and pull through loop
