Mooring

Stand by the line
Man the line, ready to receive further orders
Ease away
Pay out enough to remove most of the strain
Slack away
Pay out the line, allowing it to form an easy bight
Take in the slack
Take up all slack
Take a strain
Put the line under tension by holding back or by adding turns to the bitt
Hold the line
Take sufficient turns of the line on a bitt so that it will not give
Check the line
Hold the line, but render it enough (i.e., a foot or two at a time) when necessary to prevent parting
Double up
Run out additional lines to double all mooring lines
Single up
Take in all extra lines, leave only one in each position
Take
Give slack so that line handlers on the pier can cast off the end and bring line quickly aboard, or send ashore as needed
Cast off
Remove the line from a bitt or bollard to allow the other end to be taken in
